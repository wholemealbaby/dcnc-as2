## 4. (2 marks) Provide the reason for your design choices in A.2 within 250 words, detailing the design choices.
For the IT subnet, a switch has a connection to a server[Ethernet], a printer[Ethernet] and at least two computers[Ethernet], and connected to the router[GigaEthernet]. This is as most hardware only need a modest speed to work properly, while router needs a larger bit speed to handle larger data loads within a reasonable amount of time. To create the superconnection for the rest of the departments, each department has a similar architecture as the IT subnet, without the printer, however, the switches[GigaEthernet] of the Sales, Marketting and Administration are connected to another switch which is then connected to the router[GigaEthernet]. This is as to create the foundation of the supernetworking as with maintaining a reasonable amount of data transwre with GigaEthernet connections. All physical connections are copper Straight-Throughs as everything is housed within the same building, which means that effects such as propagation and transmission delays are low and do not warrant expensive fiber optic cables.

 The reason why we've implemented the Supernet Design 2 is that it provides substantially more capacity for users, with a focus on accurate projected growth across each department. IT is projected to have the most growth (receiving a /25 with 126 addresses), and Marketing, Administration, and Sales each receiving a /26 (62 addresses). This approach future-proofs the network against company growth and accounts for the BYOD work environment where employees typically utilise both a work computer and a personal mobile device. The supernet architecture (192.100.30.0/23) prioritises space for the IT department while keeping a sizeable amount of spare capacity for the other three departments, and aligns with RIPE NCC's utilisation policies for additional address allocation.
